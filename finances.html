Finances

The simplest model that I can think of for modeling my personal finances is to make a fixed contribution to my savings every year.

1def NetWorth(start, end, contribution):
2	net_worth = 0
3	for i in range(start, end):
4		net_worth += contribution
5	return net_worth

However, you can purchases stocks which appreciate over time. This would look like the following:

1def NetWorth(start, end, contribution, stock_price):
2	n_stocks = 0
3	for i in range(start, end):
4		n_stocks += contribution / stock_price[i]
5	return n_stocks / stock_price[end]

Another thing to consider is more customized contributions. Suppose that we represent contributions as the following:

When we are trying to estimate our net worth, its good to try to predict the inputs to our net worth model. The good thing is that we can estimate our contributions to a good degree of certainty in the short term. Much of our income comes in regular intervals (salary, equity grants).

1def RegularContributions(start, end, x, payperiod):
2	contributions = []
3	for date in range(start, end, payperiod):
4		contributions.append((date, x))
5	return contributions

Of course, our income typically changes over the course of our careers and therefore our contributions should change as we get older. One way of modeling this is a simple growth rate to our contributions which may look something like this.

1def RegularContributions(start, end, x, payperiod, yearly_rate):
2	contributions = []
3	for date in range(start, end, payperiod):
4		contribution = x * yearly_rate ** (date - start).years()
5		contributions.append((date, contribution))
6	return contributions
